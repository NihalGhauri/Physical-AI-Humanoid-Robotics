# Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics: Introduction

Welcome to the comprehensive textbook on **Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics**. This course explores the fascinating intersection of artificial intelligence and the physical world, focusing on embodied intelligence and AI systems that interact with and control physical robots.

## Course Overview

This textbook covers the design, simulation, and deployment of humanoid robots using state-of-the-art tools and frameworks:

- **ROS 2**: The standard middleware for robot communication
- **Gazebo & Unity**: Physics simulation and environment modeling
- **NVIDIA Isaac**: AI-powered perception and navigation systems
- **Vision-Language-Action (VLA)**: Integrating human language with robot action

## Target Audience

This textbook is designed for:
- Robotics researchers and engineers
- AI/ML practitioners interested in embodied systems
- Students of computer science, engineering, and robotics
- Developers working on autonomous systems

## Prerequisites

To get the most out of this course, you should have:
- Basic programming experience (Python preferred)
- Understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Familiarity with robotics concepts is helpful but not required

## Course Structure

The textbook is organized into modules that build upon each other:

1. [Robotic Nervous System](./module-1-robotic-nervous-system/intro.md) - ROS 2 architecture and fundamentals
2. [Digital Twin](./module-2-digital-twin/intro.md) - Simulation environments and sensor modeling
3. [AI-Robot Brain](./module-3-ai-robot-brain/intro.md) - Perception, navigation, and learning
4. [Vision-Language-Action](./module-4-vision-language-action/intro.md) - Human-robot interaction
5. [Capstone Project](./capstone-project/intro.md) - Complete humanoid robot implementation
